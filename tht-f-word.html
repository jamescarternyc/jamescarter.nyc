Overview

Asiah Green is a 22 year old fashion major at Stevenson University . Earlier this year, she hired me to build & brand her very own Women's fashion & lifestyle blog.

Role:

Front-End Development/Graphic Designer

When:

June 2015- Present

Visit the Live Site: thtfword.com

Wordpress Development

Asiah decided to run her blog with Wordpress, a popular content management system which makes it easy for users to update their site's content.

To work within her budget and provide a quick turn-around time, I hand-picked a premium theme to serve as the foundation for the site rather than completely build everything from scratch. Then, with a little front-end magic, I customized everything to her liking.

Focusing on Content

The site is forthright in design. Users have access to all of That F Word's social media on the left & can enjoy all of Asiah's exciting posts on the right.

I simplified the content viewing process with infinite scroll. Users simply scroll down to reveal more posts instead of having to click page by page to load older content. Infinite scroll also allows users to view the blog's content in a manner they are already accustomed to when using apps such as Twitter & Instagram.

Asiah wanted the blog to be minimalistic in design, but incredibly functional in terms of current & future usage. So, I used the sidebar as a central hub where Asiah can connect her audience with her social media presence, provide users with archives to find older content, & upload future advertisements.

Mobile-Ready

#HeyThereGorgeous

To help promote her blog, Asiah initiated a campaign via Instagram entitled, #HeyThereGorgeous. Whenever she has spare time (she's still in school), Asiah travels around the DMV area looking to capture the local women's fashion scene with her camera. To ensure all these wonderful images would be accessible on the blog, I made use of a simple light box gallery.

Identity

The wonderful thing about working with Asiah is that for the most part, she knew what she wanted and what she didn't want. This not only made designing the logo fun and easy, but was also extremely important because it allowed us to stay on schedule and budget.

Asiah knew she wanted go with a basic word mark for the logo. To add a bit of boldness & character to the page, we went with a mustard colored hand font with a dropshadow to really make it pop against the white background.

Asiah also needed some business cards to pass out during her #HeyThereGorgeous campaign. However, instead of the usual structure that includes her name and contact info, she desired a card that would extend the experience of #HeyThereGorgeous by providing instructions on what women should do after having their picture taken.

To break away from the typical routine, a card with rounded edges was chosen. Sticking with Asiah's main goal of simplicity and to ensure maximum readability. the card features a white background and a black serif font. Asiah also wanted to remind partcipants that fashion is her passion, so one of her favorite fashion quotes was inlcuded on the back of the card.

One major challenge I experienced while designing, was the lack of real-estate the minicard provided for the content. However, in a way, the same lack of space led to a clearer sense of minimalism by forcing a simpler set of instructions and a straightforward text layout.

Overall, I had a blast working on this project and look forward to working on more projects with Asiah in the future.
